About Me

I’m Daniella Ghonda, a current Computer Science student at the University of Maryland, expecting to graduate in May 2027 with a minor in Tech Entrepreneurship. I have a strong foundation in programming, with hands-on experience in languages like Java, HTML, React.js, and more. Throughout my academic and professional journey, I’ve developed a passion for creating practical, impactful projects. During my intenrship at Breakthrough Tech DC, I collaborated on GroceriScout, a website that helps users find affordable groceries in College Park. This experience honed my skills in front-end development and taught me how to deliver a product within tight timelines, presenting it to a panel of judges. As an Academic Peer Mentor for the First-Year Innovation and Research Experience (FIRE) at UMD, I guided over 35 students in launching their research projects and helped mentor five teams. I also worked on exciting research projects, including designing a colorimetric detection device integrated with a web platform, using advanced technologies like React.js. My projects include GroceriScout: Terp Edition, where I developed a price comparison tool for groceries in College Park, and a multi-threaded simulation of a social network for my Concurrency-Based Social Network Simulation project. Additionally, I created a Media Rental Manager application in Java that allows users to track and manage media rentals. I am also ISC² Certified in Cybersecurity and TestOut Security Pro certified, with a growing interest in leveraging technology to solve real-world problems. I’m always eager to take on new challenges and expand my technical skill set.
